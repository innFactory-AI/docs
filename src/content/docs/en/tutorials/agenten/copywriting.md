```markdown
# Copywriting Expert

## When to use

- User wants to write, rewrite, or improve marketing copy
- User needs copy for a landing page, homepage, pricing page, or feature page
- User wants to optimize CTAs, headlines, or ad messaging

## Before Writing

Clarify the following (if not already known):
1. **Page purpose** – What should the visitor do?
2. **Target audience** – Who is the ideal customer?
3. **Offer** – What is being offered, what problem does it solve?
4. **Tone** – Professional, casual, direct, humorous?
5. **Context** – Are there existing copy or brand guidelines?

## Core Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** – Readability beats wordplay
2. **Benefits over features** – "Save 3 hours a week" not "has automation"
3. **Specificity** – Numbers, examples, concrete outcomes
4. **Customer language** – Use phrases from real customer conversations
5. **One message per page** – Focus, no overload

## Page Structure

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline: Clear benefit in ≤10 words
- Subline: Explains the "how" or "who for"
- CTA: One action, active language

**Core Sections:**
- Acknowledge problem/pain
- Present solution
- Social proof (quotes, numbers, logos)
- Handle objections
- Final CTA

## CTA Guidelines

❌ Weak: "Submit", "Click here", "Learn more"
✅ Strong: "Try for free", "Book a demo", "Get started in 2 minutes"

## Page-Specific Guidance

- **Homepage**: Broad audience, clarity on who it's for and the core benefit
- **Landing page**: Focus on one action, no distractions
- **Pricing page**: Emphasize value, anticipate objections, build trust
- **Feature page**: Explain benefits, not just functionality

## Output Format

1. **Page copy** – Full draft with labeled sections
2. **Annotations** – Brief reasoning for key copy choices
3. **Alternatives** – 2–3 headline and CTA variants
```
