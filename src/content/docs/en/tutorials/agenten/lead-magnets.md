```markdown
# Lead Magnet Builder

## When to use

- User wants to create or optimize a lead magnet
- User is looking for ideas to grow an email list
- User wants to improve conversion rate on their opt-in page

## Core Principles

1. **Solve a specific problem** — The more concrete, the higher the conversion
2. **Buyer stage match** — Right content at the right phase
3. **High perceived value** — Should feel valuable, not spammy
4. **Natural path to product** — Lead magnet leads into the offer
5. **Quick to consume** — Deliver a quick win, don't overwhelm

## Lead Magnet Types

| Type | Strength | Best situation |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Checklist | Simple, actionable | Concrete task |
| Template | Immediately usable | Recurring work |
| Mini-guide/PDF | Comprehensive overview | Complex topic |
| Quiz | Interactive, personalized | Diagnostic need |
| Swipe File | Ready-made examples | Seeking inspiration |
| Webinar | High-value, trust-building | Expensive offer |
| Free trial | Direct product experience | SaaS |

## Lead Magnet by Buyer Stage

- **Awareness**: Educational content (What is X? How do I solve Y?)
- **Consideration**: Comparisons, checklists, templates
- **Decision**: Free trial, demo, consultation call

## Landing Page Structure

1. Clear headline: What do I get?
2. 3–5 bullet points: What exactly will I learn/receive?
3. Preview of content (screenshot, mockup)
4. Simple form (name + email is enough)
5. Privacy notice

## Distribution & Promotion

- Blog CTAs (end of post or inline)
- Exit-intent popup
- Social media teasers
- Partner sites and newsletter swaps

## Output Format

1. **Lead magnet concept** with title and content outline
2. **Landing page copy** ready to use
3. **Distribution plan** with specific channels
```
