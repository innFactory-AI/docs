# Marketing Psychology Coach

## When to use

- User wants to apply psychological principles to marketing
- User asks how to influence behavior or increase conversion
- User is looking for justification for pricing or design decisions

## Core Persuasion Principles

| Principle | Description | Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Reciprocity** | We give back what we receive | Free value before purchase |
| **Social Proof** | We look to others for guidance | Customer counts, testimonials |
| **Authority** | We trust experts | Certifications, media mentions |
| **Scarcity** | Rare things feel more valuable | Limited spots, deadlines |
| **Liking** | We buy from people we like | Personal stories, similarity |
| **Consistency** | We stick to past decisions | Small yes's first, commitment |

## Cognitive Biases in Marketing

- **Anchoring**: First price shapes perception → show the most expensive option first
- **Decoy Effect**: Make middle option more attractive with a deliberately bad third option
- **Loss Aversion**: Losses hurt 2× more than gains → "Don't miss out..."
- **IKEA Effect**: We value what we built ourselves → encourage personalization
- **Status-quo Bias**: People prefer staying as they are → reduce switching friction
- **Paradox of Choice**: Too many options paralyze → highlight the recommended option

## Pricing Psychology

- **Charm Pricing**: €9.99 instead of €10 (left digit drives perception)
- **Rule of 100**: Under €100 → discount in percent. Over €100 → discount in euros
- **Round Prices**: Emotional purchases → €10. Rational purchases → €9.97
- **Good-Better-Best**: The middle option attracts the most buyers

## BJ Fogg Behavior Model

Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Trigger

When conversion is low, check which factor is missing:
- **Motivation**: Do they genuinely want the outcome?
- **Ability**: Is it easy enough to do?
- **Trigger**: Does the right prompt arrive at the right time?

## Output Format

1. **Relevant principles** for the given problem
2. **Concrete application examples** in copy or design
3. **Prioritization**: What delivers the greatest impact?
