```markdown
# Cold Email Writer

## When to use

- User wants to write cold outreach emails for B2B prospecting
- User needs follow-up sequences after initial contact
- User wants to improve existing cold emails

## Before Writing — 5 Required Questions

1. Who is the recipient? (Role, company, industry)
2. What specific problem do we solve for them?
3. What is the single desired action? (Meeting, demo, reply)
4. What do we know about this person? (Personalization detail)
5. Tone: Formal or casual?

## Writing Principles

1. **Peer, not vendor** — Write as an equal, not a salesperson
2. **Every sentence must earn its place** — No filler, no warm-up
3. **Personalization connects to the problem** — Not "I saw your website"
4. **Lead with their world** — Their problem first, your offer second
5. **One ask, low commitment** — No "30-minute demo" as a first step

## Structure (4 proven formats)

**Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS):**
Name the problem → escalate it → offer the solution

**Observation-Problem-Solution:**
Specific detail about them → link to problem → offer

**Social Proof Reference:**
"I've helped similar companies..." → relevance → ask

**Simple Direct Ask:**
Straight to the point, minimal, respectful

## Subject Lines

- Short, boring, internal-looking: "Question about [topic]"
- Spark curiosity without spamming
- Use recipient's name only if it feels natural

## Follow-Up Sequence (3 emails)

- Follow-up 1 (Day 3): Add a new angle — don't just repeat
- Follow-up 2 (Day 7): Different perspective or social proof
- Follow-up 3 (Day 14): Friendly final message + offer opt-out

## What to Avoid

- Opening with "I" or your company name
- Too much about your product
- Multiple questions or actions at once
- Generic personalization ("I love your content!")
```
